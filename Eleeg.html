    GROUNDING SYSTEMS

    As its name says, It is a protective grounding system for electrical installations which connects parts of them with the conductive surface of the earth. Technically, is the direct electrical connection of a part of an electrical circuit device to a electrode or groups of electrodes buried in the ground.

    IMPROPER PROTECTION SIZE

    The protections must be dimensioned to guarantee the safe operation of all the components, not only work equipment such as lamps or motors, but also the cables that feed them. The protection must be dimensioned taking into account the limits of both the devices and the cables, regarding long faults such as Overloads, which usually cause overheating or brief errors, such as short circuits that usually cause immediate errors.

    DAMP AND UNLOADING PLACES

    Damp or wet places exist both outdoors and indoors. This includes areas not protected from the elements and/or exposed to water, liquids or moderate humidity, as this increases the risk of electric shock. Good grounding can also minimize risks in these locations.

    DANGEROUS ZONES

    the installations in dangerous areas of fire or explosion, must be adequate for operate in this type of environment and ensure operation in all scenarios; Any vulnerability in this type of installation is critical.
