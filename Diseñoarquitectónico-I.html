Architectural Design

Introduction

Architectural drawing is the way to express ideas through scaled graphic representations, including the depiction of buildings, landscapes, and urban planning, following architectural design standards and regulations such as NSR-10 (Seismic Resistant Code) and current drafting standards.

At Benjamín Herrera Technical School, students in this specialty receive theoretical, technical, and technological knowledge that enables them to develop graphic solutions both manually and digitally using various tools available in the market. In addition, students are involved in academic processes that allow them to illustrate architectural elements through various graphic representations such as perspectives, models, orthogonal projections, sketches, computer-aided designs, blueprints, and 3D renderings. These skills enable them to create realistic representations of architectural elements, materials, finishes, and more—contributing to their training in this field.

Objective

To effectively contribute to the comprehensive training of technical students in architectural design through the study of topics focused on design, architecture, and construction, based on the development of skills, specific knowledge, general job competencies, and citizenship skills.

Student Profile

    ♦ The student in the Architectural Design specialty is characterized by being responsible, creative, patient, dedicated to their work, capable of solving real problems with resourcefulness and common sense, and demonstrating the ethical attitude required to reflect this in their work, community, personal, and professional life.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the Architectural Design specialty can continue their professional education path in related disciplines such as Architecture, Architectural Drafting, Graphic Design, Industrial Design, Fashion Design, Teaching of Design, Advertising, Interior Design and Decoration, and Engineering in general.
