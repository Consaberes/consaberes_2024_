Biotechnology - Food Agroindustry

Introduction

The specialty in Food Agroindustry at the Benjamín Herrera Technical College aims to train high school graduates with skills in food production. The purpose is to guarantee the production of safe and high-quality food products from the transformation of raw materials such as fruits, vegetables, bakery products, meats, and dairy products.

The program is carried out during the tenth and eleventh grades of technical secondary education. Four lines of work are taught: fruits, vegetables, and baking in the first year, and meats and dairy products in the second year. In addition, to obtain certification from SENA and a degree from the college, students of this specialty must complete a practical stage in companies in the agroindustrial sector.

Objective

Train high school graduates at the Benjamín Herrera Technical College for the agribusiness sector food production through the development of specific work skills capable of performing successfully in the workplace and its continuity in higher education.

Student profile

  • ♦ Maintain excellent personal hygiene
  • ♦ Supervise the operations to which the food is subjected
  • ♦ Maintain the quality of the materials through correct control systems
  • ♦ Properly operate process control instruments
  • ♦ Evaluate appropriate ranges for process variables
  • ♦ Estimate food quality and safety to protect consumers
  • ♦ Adopt new technologies and foster strong interpersonal skills

Occupational field

The Food Agroindustry Technician will be able to work in industries related to the processing and transformation of raw materials and inputs for the fruit, bakery, meat, and dairy lines. They are also trained in areas like process control and food and beverage processing machines.
