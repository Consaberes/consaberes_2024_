Biotechnology - Food Agroindustry

    Introduction

    The Food Agroindustry specialty at Benjamín Herrera Technical School aims to train students in food production skills. The purpose is to ensure the production of safe, high-quality food products through the transformation of raw materials such as fruits, vegetables, bakery products, meat, and dairy. The program is carried out during the tenth and eleventh grades of technical secondary education. It includes four work areas: fruits, vegetables, and bakery in the first year; meats and dairy in the second year. Additionally, to obtain certification from SENA and the school's diploma, students must complete a practical stage in agroindustrial companies.

    Objective

    To train students at Benjamín Herrera Technical School for the food agroindustry sector by developing specific job skills that enable them to succeed in the workforce and pursue higher education.

    Student Profile

  • ♦ Maintain excellent personal hygiene

    ♦ Supervise the processes food products go through

    ♦ Ensure the quality of materials involved in processing by correctly applying control systems designed for raw materials, processing, and the final product

    ♦ Properly operate process control instruments to maintain desired conditions

    ♦ Evaluate the appropriate ranges of variables involved in processing based on the desired product and raw material

    ♦ Assess the quality and safety of processed food to ensure consumer health

    ♦ Embrace new technologies, deep learning, and interaction with all production sectors, while strengthening interpersonal relationships in the workplace

    • Job Opportunities

    A technician in Food Agroindustry can work in industries related to the processing and transformation of raw materials in the fruit-vegetable, bakery, meat, and dairy sectors. They are also trained as process and machine control operators for food and beverage production.
